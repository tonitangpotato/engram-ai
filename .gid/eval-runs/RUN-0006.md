## Why this run exists

This run does **NOT** seek a new score. It runs the **same retrieval over the same DB** with new instrumentation that splits hit@5 by LoCoMo question category and surfaces a cleaner cat 1-4 headline.

## Findings

1. **Multi-hop is fully broken (0/3).** All 3 cat=1 queries miss:
   - q4 (D2:8): planned Abstract, downgraded to FTS; gold not in top-5.
   - q5 (D1:5): planned Factual; ranking miss.
   - q8 (D2:14, D3:13): planned Factual; ranking miss.
   None of the three are admission gaps — gold turns are in the substrate. The plan dispatcher has no real multi-hop traversal; it falls through to single-shot Factual/Abstract and loses.
2. **Temporal is the high-water mark (85.7%).** The single Temporal miss (q2, D1:12) is the same one RUN-0005 flagged as a query↔content lexical+semantic gap. Temporal queries usually pin a date or relative time, and FTS+embedding handles those well.
3. **Single-hop underperforms (33.3%).** This is surprising — Single-hop should be the easy cohort. 6 of 9 misses split into:
   - 3 ranking misses where gold turn is in candidates but outranked (q14, q19, q20)
   - 1 Affective plan with `outcome=no_cognitive_state` (q18) — Affective plan is shipping a no-op
   - 1 empty Hybrid (q13)
   - 1 Abstract downgrade (q15)
   The Affective `no_cognitive_state` outcome is a structural bug: the plan kind is selected but the executor has no cognitive-state index to query. Filing as a candidate issue (see "Next").
4. **Cat=5 Adversarial (40%) is meaningless as a retrieval metric.** The gold label is "the model should refuse / say it doesn't know". Whether retrieval surfaces a related turn is orthogonal to whether the downstream answer is correct. Excluding cat=5 from the headline is justified. **Going forward, report `hit@5 (cat 1-4)` as the primary number.**
5. **Hybrid plan has 100% empty rate (0/2 with n=2).** Both Hybrid dispatches return `empty_result_set`. This is the ISS-063 fallback contract working as designed (no candidates → empty, not crash) but the fact that 2/2 land here means Hybrid plan selection is too aggressive or the Hybrid executor is not wired to the FTS/vector backends. Worth digging.
